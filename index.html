Alan Turing

Mathematician and computer science pioneer

Family

Turing was born in Maida Vale, London.

Very early in life, Turing showed signs of the genius that he was later to display prominently.

School

Between January 1922 and 1926, Turing was educated at Hazelhurst Preparatory School.

In 1928, aged 16, Turing encountered Albert Einstein's work; not only did he grasp it, but it is possible that he managed to deduce Einstein's questioning of Newton's laws of motion from a text in which this was never made explicit.

University and work on computability

Turing studied as an undergraduate from 1931 to 1934 at King's College, Cambridge.

In 1936, Turing published his paper "On Computable Numbers, with an Application to the Entscheidungsproblem".

In June 1938, he obtained his PhD from the Department of Mathematics at Princeton.
